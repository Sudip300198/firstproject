Ayodhya Ram Mandir: More photos of Ram Lalla placed for consecration ceremony released; Check latest pics here

ET Online. Last Update on 19 Jan 2024, 1500 hrs

Ram Mandir Inauguration: The 51-inch idol of Lord Ram, sculpted by Arun Yogiraj, was placed in Ayodhya's Ram Temple for the 'pran pratishtha' ceremony. PM Modi will perform the puja for the 'Pran Pratistha' on January 22. He has requested the public not to visit Ayodhya on that day and has encouraged lighting a diya in every Indian home.

The highly anticipated consecration ceremony of the Ram Temple in Ayodhya is drawing near, with the idol of Lord Ram already placed within the inner sanctum. Crafted by renowned sculptor Arun Yogiraj, the 51-inch idol depicts Lord Ram as a five-year-old child standing on a lotus, all made from black stone. The ceremony, scheduled for January 22, has garnered widespread attention, with Prime Minister Narendra Modi set to perform the 'Pran Pratishtha' rituals.


Idol Placement and Details


The 'Ram Lalla' idol, weighing 1.5 tonnes, was positioned in the sanctum sanctorum of Ayodhya's Ram Temple on Thursday. Accompanied by prayers and rituals, the ceremony included 'Ganesh Pujan' and 'Varun Pujan' . Covered with a veil, the first glimpse of the idol was revealed during the placement proceedings. The meticulous craftsmanship by Arun Yogiraj is evident, capturing Lord Ram in a serene pose atop a lotus.

North India to reel under fog, cold wave for 3-5 more days

IMD predicts 3-5 more days of cold wave and foggy conditions in north and northwest India due to lack of active western disturbance, El Nino conditions, and strong jet stream winds. Jet streams are bands of strong wind impacting weather. Minimum temperatures have been below 4 degree Celsius since Jan 12.

NEW DELHI: New Delhi: Predicting three to five more days of cold and fog conditions in north and northwest parts of the country, the India Meteorological Department (IMD) has attributed the current severe weather to three reasons including lack of any active Western Disturbance (WD) over northwest India during Dec and Jan, El Nino conditions (warm ocean temperatures) over the equatorial Pacific Ocean and strong jet stream winds.
